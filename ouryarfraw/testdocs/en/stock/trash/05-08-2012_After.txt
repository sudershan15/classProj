Log In By Carla Mozee, MarketWatch LOS ANGELES (MarketWatch) — Wall Street late Tuesday will look for Walt Disney Co. to report growth in quarterly earnings and sales from a year ago, and Demand Media Inc. is forecast to post a pullback in earnings but a rise in sales. Disney /quotes/zigman/245568/quotes/nls/dis DIS +1.10%  is expected to report fiscal second-quarter earnings of 55 cents a share, up 12% from the same quarter a year ago, according to a poll of analysts taken by FactSet Research. Revenue is forecast to rise 5% to $9.56 billion. Investors will watch for any word from Disney about its outlook as the studio comes off the record $207.4 million opening weekend in North America for “The Avengers”. Read more on Avengers. Earnings at Demand Media Inc. /quotes/zigman/3620702/quotes/nls/dmd DMD -3.88%  , a provider of online content and social media services, are expected to slip a penny a share to 5 cents a share from the first quarter a year ago. Revenue, however, is expected to increase 5% to $80 million. Ahead of the late trading session, U.S. stocks dropped as investors concerned about a flare-up in Greece’s debt crisis flocked to the U.S. dollar and Treasury bonds.
