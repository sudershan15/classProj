Log In By MarketWatch CHICAGO (MarketWatch) — The following companies made notable moves in U.S. trading on Tuesday: Gainers Arena Pharmaceuticals Inc. /quotes/zigman/83618/quotes/nls/arna ARNA +25.74%  were up 19% ahead of a scheduled meeting of a U.S. Food and Drug Administration advisory panel that will discuss whether to recommend approval of Arena’s weight-loss drug lorcaserin. Cognizant Technology Solutions Corp. /quotes/zigman/70561/quotes/nls/ctsh CTSH +7.10%  rebounded to advance 6.2% Tuesday after William Baird & Co. raised its rating on the stock to outperform from neutral. On Monday, the shares sank more than 19% after the company trimmed its 2012 revenue target to at least $7.34 billion, below the Wall Street estimate of $7.55 billion. Read more in Tech Stocks. OfficeMax Inc. /quotes/zigman/359604/quotes/nls/omx OMX +11.14%  shares were up nearly 11% after the retailer reported better-than-expected first-quarter earnings. Quality Distribution Inc. /quotes/zigman/87548/quotes/nls/qlty QLTY +12.97%  shares were up 9.5% after the company entered into a deal to acquire the assets of Wylie Bice Trucking LLC and RM Resources LLC for as much as $98.3 million. Vitamin Shoppe Inc. /quotes/zigman/562145/quotes/nls/vsi VSI +15.26%  shares were up 12% on better-than-expected first-quarter results. Decliners Dendreon Corp. /quotes/zigman/83175/quotes/nls/dndn DNDN -25.15%   shares were down 24%, the day after the biotech firm issued a weaker-than-expected earnings report. Discovery Communications Inc. /quotes/zigman/112277/quotes/nls/disca DISCA -6.07%  was down 6.2% after the cable-television programmer’s report of a 28% decline in first-quarter earnings, driven by losses from OWN, its joint venture with Oprah Winfrey.
